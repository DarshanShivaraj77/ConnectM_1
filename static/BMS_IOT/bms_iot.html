Mounting Recommendations
Installation
🔌 Connecting Wires
  • Wires should be fastened to other wires or non-moving parts. Avoid heat-emitting and moving objects near the wires.
  • Connections should not be exposed. If factory isolation was removed while connecting wires, it should be re-applied.
  • If wires are placed in the exterior or in places where they can be damaged or exposed to heat, humidity or dirt, additional isolation should be applied.
  • Wires cannot be connected to the board computers or control units.
⚡ Connecting Power Source
  • Ensure that after the vehicle computer falls asleep, power is still available on chosen wire. This may happen in 5 to 30 minutes depending on the vehicle.
  • When the module is connected, measure voltage again to ensure it did not decrease.
  • It is recommended to connect to the main power cable in the fuse box.
🔑 Connecting Ignition Wire
  • Verify it is a real ignition wire — power should not disappear after starting the engine.
  • Check this is not an ACC wire (when key is in first position, most vehicle electronics are available).
  • Check that power is still available when you turn off any vehicle device.
  • Ignition is connected to the ignition relay output. Any relay with power output when ignition is ON may be used as an alternative.
🪛 Connecting Ground Wire
  • Ground wire is connected to the vehicle frame or metal parts fixed to the frame.
  • If the wire is fixed with a bolt, the loop must be connected to the end of the wire.
  • For better contact, scrub paint from the spot where the loop is going to be connected.

Safety Information
⚠️ Important

This section contains information on how to operate MELI MOBILITY – BTS 400 safely. By following these requirements and recommendations, you will avoid dangerous situations. Read these instructions carefully and follow them strictly before operating the device.

  • The device uses SELV limited power source. Nominal voltage is +12 V DC. Allowed range: +9 V to +100 V DC.
  • To avoid mechanical damage, transport the device in an impact-proof package. Before usage, place the device so its LED indicators are visible.
  • When connecting cables to the vehicle, the appropriate jumpers of the power supply should be disconnected.
  • Before dismounting the device from the vehicle, the cable connection must be disconnected.
  • The device is designed to be mounted in a zone of limited access, inaccessible to the operator.
  • This device is not designed as a navigational device for boats.
Do not disassemble the device. If the device is damaged, power supply cables are not isolated or isolation is damaged, DO NOT touch the device before unplugging the power supply.
All wireless data transferring devices produce interference that may affect other devices placed nearby.
The device must be connected only by qualified personnel.
The device must be firmly fastened in a predefined location.
Programming must be performed using a PC with autonomic power supply.
Installation and/or handling during a lightning storm is prohibited.
The device is susceptible to water and humidity.
