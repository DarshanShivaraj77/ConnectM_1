1. Core Platform Scope

Decision
The platform is defined as an Asset Lifecycle Management (ALM) SaaS for IoT-enabled assets across batteries, vehicles, HVAC, and medical equipment. Focus areas include warranty risk, operational efficiency, and lifecycle intelligence rather than generic fleet management.

2. Asset as Source of Truth

Decision
Assets are treated as immutable entities with a unique identity. All telemetry, warranty, claims, transfers, and operational history attach to the asset. Assets can appear in multiple workspaces but are never duplicated.

3. Multi-Tenancy & Data Isolation

Decision
Organizations are strict tenants with isolation enforced at the data layer. Products are global once approved; assets are scoped to organizations with auditable transfer history.

4. Workspace Model

Decision
Workspaces are observational lenses, not ownership containers. Three types are defined: Actual, Hierarchy, and Operational. Workspaces define KPI boundaries and collaboration scope.

5. Operational Workspaces

Decision
Operational workspaces are problem-driven and time-bound. Any user with access to at least 10 assets can create one. Assets enter via rules or manual pinning.

6. Product & Global Catalog

Decision
OEMs onboard products and variants locally. ConnectM approves products to a global catalog. Once approved, products become reusable and immutable.

7. IoT & Rule Engine

Decision
IoT telemetry is configured at product level. Rules are categorized into alerts, events, operational KPIs, and reports. Rule evaluation drives asset inclusion, alerts, and warranty logic.

8. Dashboards Strategy

Decision
Three dashboard types are defined: - Asset Dashboard (single asset truth) - Workspace Dashboard (aggregated KPIs) - User KPI Dashboard (accountability) Landing page is always the workspace boundary dashboard.

9. Claims & Warranty Model

Decision
Claims are raised against assets and can be initiated from workspaces. Approval, rejection, and reasoning are fully auditable. Workspace-level claim correlation is a first-class view.

10. Pricing Philosophy

Decision
Pricing is aligned to warranty savings rather than users or devices. Model includes base platform fee, per-asset intelligence fee, and success-based savings share.

11. Collaboration & External Users

Decision
Workspace owners can invite internal and external users. External users receive scoped access limited to workspace assets. Access is time-bound and revocable.

12. Documentation & Execution

Decision
Conversation history is preserved as a design intent archive. Execution artifacts include Jira-ready user stories, glossary, UI baseline, and decision logs.
